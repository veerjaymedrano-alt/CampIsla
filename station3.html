Eco IS-tability

Our team studied the mangrove region to identify all the mangrove tree species that grow along the beach. We captured images of the plant's roots and leaves together with all other plant components while we documented our findings on the designated worksheet.

I found that mangroves have unique traits which allow them to survive in saltwater environments. The trees use their roots to create protective structures which shield the coast from both erosion and strong ocean currents. The activity showed me that mangroves provide protective breeding grounds for aquatic life. The mangrove tree requires all its components to maintain its existence. Mangroves require protection because they deliver essential environmental advantages for our ecosystem. All people from different cultural backgrounds must share the duty of saving mangrove forest ecosystems. Mangroves create natural protection for coastal regions while establishing habitats that support multiple animal species. Everyone must work to safeguard mangrove ecosystems.
